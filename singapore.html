Famous Places In singapore

Merlion Park

The Merlion Park, which is located in the Downtown Core district near Central Business District of Singapore, is one of the country’s sought-after tourist sites. As the name indicates, the park situates two statues of the Merlion, a mythical creature with a lion's head and the body and tail of a fish.

Garden by the Bay

Garden by the Bay is a huge natural park and botanic garden in Singapore. Spanning an extensive area of 101 hectares, the garden is the home to approximately 400,000 plants, which is divided into three parts including Bay Central Garden, Bay East Garden, and Bay South Garden.

Marina Bay Sands

Regarded as Singapore’s most iconic hotel, Marina Bay Sands is a magnificent resort complex consisting of a high-end luxury 55-storey hotel, an opulent shopping mall with a canal running through, the ArtScience Museum, and the Marina Bay Sands Skypark Observation Deck where enables visitors to look out over the entire city.

China Town

Singapore Chinatown also offers a great blend of cultural diversity and it is the only Chinatown all over the world to have three different worship spots from three cultures: a Buddhist temple, a mosque and a Hindu temple along a single street.

Sentosa Island

Located not far away from the city centre to the South, Sentosa is a beautiful island where offer endless fun from dawn till dusk for people of all age. Being the place of the sun, the sand, and the sea, Sentosa is not only famous for beach activities but also a paradise of amusement.

Orchard Road

As one of the major hubs in Asia, it comes as no surprise that Singapore is also a busy shopping city where houses multiple stores of global brands and numerous outlets in various class. For many people, they can do nothing when travelling to Singapore but shopping.

Famous Local Foods In singapore

Laksa

Laksa is another popular noodle dish that’s a staple in Singapore food culture.Think of Laksa as the love child of spicy curry and noodle soup, born from Peranakan heritage.

Bak Kut Teh

Bak Kut Teh means meat bone tea, though there is no tea in the soup. This peppery pork rib soup is slow simmered with garlic, white pepper and herbs until the meat is fall off the bone tender. Add rice, fried dough sticks and preserved vegetables for a hearty meal.

Chilli Crab

Spicy, sweet, messy and glorious. Chilli Crab is the life of the Singaporean food party. Imagine fresh mud crab stir fried in a rich, tangy tomato chili gravy that is more savory than spicy. Served with soft steamed or crispy fried mantou buns to soak up the sauce, it is an absolute feast and a hands on experience.

Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is another well loved Singapore food breakfast, perfectly portioned to keep you full, though many enjoy it for lunch too. This fragrant Malay dish features coconut rice served with fried chicken, spicy sambal, anchovies, peanuts, cucumber and egg. Often wrapped in banana leaves, it is a full meal in one convenient package that is equally delicious for breakfast, lunch or dinner.

Claypot Rice

Claypot rice is a beloved local dish featuring marinated chicken, Chinese sausage, salted fish, and rice cooked over a traditional charcoal fire. The result? Smoky, crispy rice at the bottom of the pot, rich umami flavors, and a comforting, hearty meal that’s perfect for sharing.
