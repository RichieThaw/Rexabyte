Welcome to Ayeyarwady Division

Ayeyarwady, known as Myanmar's rice bowl, is famous for its lush delta landscapes, peaceful beaches, and vibrant riverside culture. From charming Pathein city to pristine Ngwe Saung Beach, this region offers a perfect mix of tradition and relaxation.

Best Places To Go !

Chaung Tha Beach

Chaung Tha Beach is a lively and fun destination, popular among local families and young travelers. The beach is lined with small seafood stalls and shops, creating a friendly, relaxed vibe. Visitors enjoy fresh seafood, swimming, and boat rides to nearby islands.A lively and popular beach, loved by locals for its fresh seafood, fun water activities, and relaxed vibes. Perfect for family and friends.

Ngwe Saung

Ngwe Saung Beach is known for its long, clean white sands and crystal-clear water. It offers a quiet, romantic atmosphere, perfect for couples and peaceful retreats. Many luxury resorts and beachfront restaurants make it a top seaside destination in Myanmar. A serene white-sand beach offering luxury resorts and peaceful sunsets. Ideal for couples and quiet getaways.

Pathein City

Capital of Ayeyarwady, Pathein is famous for its colorful handmade umbrellas and rich river culture. A great place to explore markets and local life. The city has lively markets, delicious street food, and a charming riverside promenade. It serves as the cultural and economic heart of the Ayeyarwady delta region.

Shwemokhtaw

An ancient and important pagoda in Pathein, originally built by Mon kings, representing centuries of faith and architecture. Shwemokhtaw Pagoda is one of the most sacred sites in Pathein, with a golden spire shining in the skyline. Originally built by Mon kings, it represents centuries of faith and tradition. Visitors enjoy peaceful moments and panoramic views over the city.

Meinmahla Kyun

A mangrove wildlife sanctuary famous for crocodiles and rich bird life. Great for eco-tours and adventurous travelers. Meinmahla Kyun is a famous mangrove sanctuary, home to crocodiles and many rare bird species. It offers boat tours through dense mangrove forests, perfect for eco-tourism lovers. The sanctuary is a vital place for biodiversity and local fishing communities.

Goyangyi Island

A small island near Ngwe Saung, known for its peaceful beaches and clear blue water. Great for snorkeling, swimming, and relaxing without big crowds. Visitors can enjoy hiking to the hill for beautiful panoramic sea views.

Local Famous Dishes !

Mohinga

Mohinga is Myanmar’s national dish and a popular breakfast in Ayeyarwady. It is a warm, fragrant rice noodle soup with fish broth, flavored with lemongrass and banana stem. Fresh river fish gives Ayeyarwady’s mohinga a rich, special taste. Rice noodle fish soup, considered Myanmar's national dish. Ayeyarwady’s version uses fresh river fish for a stronger flavor.

Pathein Halawa is a traditional Burmese sweet treat made with semolina, sugar, milk, and ghee. It's flavored with cardamom and optionally saffron, and can be garnished with chopped nuts. This rich and indulgent dessert has a smooth, thick texture and a delightful nutty aroma.

Pathein Halawa

Nga Tha Lauk Hin

resh river fish caught in the Ayeyarwady delta is cooked with tomatoes, onions, and local spices. The curry has a rich, savory taste and is usually enjoyed with steamed rice. It is a daily favorite in delta homes and highlights the fresh fish of the region.

Ngapi Yay is a savory fermented fish paste dip served with fresh or boiled vegetables. It is a daily favorite in delta homes, adding bold flavor to simple meals. Locals love to share it during communal feasts or family dinners. Fermented fish paste dip served with fresh and boiled vegetables. A must-have for local meals in the delta.

Ngapi Yay

Fried Prawns

Ayeyarwady is famous for its big, fresh river prawns, perfect for frying. The prawns are lightly seasoned and fried until crispy and golden. They are often enjoyed as a snack or main dish by the sea or riverside. Fresh delta prawns fried crispy with spices. Popular snack or main dish at beach areas and riverside towns.
