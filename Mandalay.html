Welcome to Mandalay

Discover the cultural heart of Myanmar! From ancient temples and royal palaces to stunning sunsets at U Bein Bridge, Mandalay offers history, beauty, and tradition in every corner. Start your journey here and explore the true spirit of Myanmar.

Best Place To Go

Mandalay Palace

Mandalay Palace was the last royal palace of Burmese kings. It is surrounded by a large moat and high walls. The original palace was destroyed in World War II, but it has been reconstructed. It reflects traditional Burmese royal architecture and history.

Mahar Myat Muni

The Maha Myat Muni Pagoda, southwest of **Mandalay**, Myanmar, is a revered Buddhist pilgrimage site. It houses the **Mahamuni Buddha Image**, believed to be one of five likenesses made during the Buddha's lifetime. King Bodawpaya brought the image from the Rakhine state in 1784. Devotees continuously apply **gold leaf** to the statue, and a significant daily ritual is the early morning **face-washing ceremony**.

U Bein Bridge

U Bein Bridge is the world’s longest teakwood bridge, located in Amarapura. It stretches over 1.2 kilometers across Taungthaman Lake. The bridge is most beautiful at sunrise and sunset. Locals and monks walk across it daily, giving it cultural charm.

Kuthodaw Pagoda

Kuthodaw Pagoda is known as the “World’s Largest Book.” It has 729 stone slabs with Buddhist scriptures carved on them. These slabs are housed in white stupas around the golden pagoda. It was built by King Mindon in the 1860s.

Mya Thein Tan

Mya Thein Tan Pagoda, also known as Hsinbyume Pagoda, is a stunning white pagoda in Mingun near Mandalay. Built in 1816 by King Bagyidaw in memory of his wife, it is famous for its unique wave-like terraces that represent Mount Meru. The bright white structure and beautiful design make it a popular spot for visitors and photographers.

Sagaing Hill

Sagaing Hill is a peaceful place filled with pagodas and monasteries. It is a center for Buddhist learning and meditation. From the top, you get beautiful views of the Irrawaddy River. U Min Thonze Pagoda with 45 Buddha statues is a highlight.

Pyin Oo Lwin

Pyin Oo Lwin is a cool-climate hill town from the British colonial era. It is known for flower gardens, waterfalls, and old British buildings. The Botanical Garden is the most popular place to visit. Horse carriages and strawberries are also famous here.

Famous Local Dishes!

Mee Shay

Mee Shay is a famous food in Mandalay made of rice noodles mixed with meat sauce. The sauce of Mee Shay includes coriander soya bean sauce, garlic, and chili oil. Like other Burmese noodles, Mee Shay is cook with the broth simmered from pig bone to create a strong perfume for the dish.

Mont Ti is the famous traditional food of Mandalay that people in Myanmar love very much and enjoy regularly for their breakfast. Mont Ti in Mandalay traditionally made with fresh thin rice noodles, meat or chicken curry, and onion. There are different sizes such as large, medium and small to choose from. Normally, Mont Ti is served with red chili paste and oil creating the yellow color.

Mont Ti

PaYitKyaw

Perhaps you have never heard about this name before. Nevertheless, fried cricket is an amazing option for you whenever traveling to Mandalay. It may strange with many people but is one of the favorite seasonal food in Mandalay. The cricket is fried with mixed spices create an unforgettable flavor. However, it is served only in winter and rainy season.

Htoe Mont is a traditional Mandalay dessert made from glutinous rice flour, coconut, raisins, and nuts. It has a sweet, chewy texture and is often served during festivals and special occasions. Its rich taste and sticky consistency make it a favorite treat for both locals and visitors. A sticky rice cake filled with coconut, raisins, and nuts, usually enjoyed during festive occasions.

Htoe Mont

Khotaung Mont Ti

Khotaung Mont Ti (ခိုတောင်မုန့်တီ) is a famous noodle dish that originated from Khotaung village near Mandalay. It is made with thin rice noodles served in a light, flavorful fish broth, usually topped with shredded fish, crispy fritters, and fresh herbs. Known for its refreshing and delicate taste, this dish is loved by locals and perfect for a light meal.

Fermented tea leaf salad, which is loved all over Myanmar, but Mandalay is especially known for its flavorful versions. In ancient times, fermented tea leaves were used as a peace symbol or as a peace offering between kingdoms at war. Now, a lahpet tray is the traditional expression of hospitality offering to houseguests.
