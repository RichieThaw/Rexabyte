Welcome to Mandalay

Discover the cultural heart of Myanmar! From ancient temples and royal palaces to stunning sunsets at U Bein Bridge, Mandalay offers history, beauty, and tradition in every corner. Start your journey here and explore the true spirit of Myanmar.

Best Place To Go

Mandalay Palace

Mandalay Palace was the last royal palace of Burmese kings. It is surrounded by a large moat and high walls. The original palace was destroyed in World War II, but it has been reconstructed. It reflects traditional Burmese royal architecture and history.

Mahar Myat Muni

The Maha Myat Muni Pagoda, southwest of **Mandalay**, Myanmar, is a revered Buddhist pilgrimage site. It houses the **Mahamuni Buddha Image**, believed to be one of five likenesses made during the Buddha's lifetime. King Bodawpaya brought the image from the Rakhine state in 1784. Devotees continuously apply **gold leaf** to the statue, and a significant daily ritual is the early morning **face-washing ceremony**.

U Bein Bridge

U Bein Bridge is the world’s longest teakwood bridge, located in Amarapura. It stretches over 1.2 kilometers across Taungthaman Lake. The bridge is most beautiful at sunrise and sunset. Locals and monks walk across it daily, giving it cultural charm.

Kuthodaw Pagoda

Kuthodaw Pagoda is known as the “World’s Largest Book.” It has 729 stone slabs with Buddhist scriptures carved on them. These slabs are housed in white stupas around the golden pagoda. It was built by King Mindon in the 1860s.

Mya Thein Tan

Mya Thein Tan Pagoda, also known as Hsinbyume Pagoda, is a stunning white pagoda in Mingun near Mandalay. Built in 1816 by King Bagyidaw in memory of his wife, it is famous for its unique wave-like terraces that represent Mount Meru. The bright white structure and beautiful design make it a popular spot for visitors and photographers.

Sagaing Hill

Sagaing Hill is a peaceful place filled with pagodas and monasteries. It is a center for Buddhist learning and meditation. From the top, you get beautiful views of the Irrawaddy River. U Min Thonze Pagoda with 45 Buddha statues is a highlight.

Pyin Oo Lwin

Pyin Oo Lwin is a cool-climate hill town from the British colonial era. It is known for flower gardens, waterfalls, and old British buildings. The Botanical Garden is the most popular place to visit. Horse carriages and strawberries are also famous here.

Famous Local Dishes!

Mee Shay

A Mandalay favorite, Mee Shay is rice noodles with rich pork-bone broth, coriander soybean sauce, garlic, chili oil, and tender meat.

Mont Ti

Mandalay’s beloved breakfast, Mont Ti features thin rice noodles with curry, onion, and chili oil, offering a flavorful, hearty start.

PaYitKyaw

A unique Mandalay seasonal snack, fried crickets spiced to perfection deliver a crunchy, unforgettable taste, especially in rainy or winter seasons.

Khotaung Mont Ti

Khotaung Mont Ti is a Mandalay noodle dish with thin rice noodles in light fish broth, topped with shredded fish, fritters, and herbs.

Tea leaf salad

Tea leaf salad, a beloved Mandalay specialty, mixes fermented leaves with nuts and spices, once a peace symbol and now a sign of hospitality.

Htoe Mont

This Mandalay festival dessert blends glutinous rice flour, coconut, raisins, and nuts into a sweet, sticky, chewy treat everyone loves.
