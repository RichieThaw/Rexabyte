Welcome to Yangon!

Yangon known as Rangoon ,is the largest city in Myanmar and was the country's capital until 2006.It located in Southern Myanmar, near the Yangon river and population is over 5 million people.Language is Burmese and main religion is Buddhism.It remains the commercial ,cultural and historical heart of the nation.

Best Places To Go!

Sule

Sule Pagoda is a prominent and sacred Buddhist pagoda located in the heart of Yangon ,Myanmar. It's not only a religious site but also a historical and cultural lankmark withe great significance in the country's history,it is believed to be over 2,000 years old, making it one of the oldest pagodas in Myanmar.

Shwedagon

Shwedagon Pagoda is a majestic stupa that stands on Singuttara Hill in Yangon.It's believed to be over 2,600 years old, making it the oldest Buddhist stupa in the world.The pagoda is covered with real gold plates and top is encrusted with thousands of diamonds,rubies, sapphires,and precious stones.The crown of pagoda is topped with a 76-carat diamonds.

Botataung

Botataung pagoda is located near Yangon ,specifically in the South Okkalapa Township area. It is believed that the pagoda was built to honor and pray for blessings of about one thousand militory officers.The pagoda focuses on its majestic golden stupa facade,gleaming in daylight.One image shows exterior and entrance,with Yangon River nearby ,hinting at the hollow interior where relics are enshrined.

Kyauktawgyi

Kyauk Taw Gyi pagoda, known as pagoda of the Great Marble Image is located atop Mindhamma Hill in Insein Township, Yangon about 15km northwest of downtown and near Yangon. It is carved a single block of white marble.The statue stands approximately 37 feet(11m)tall,24 feet wide,and weighs around 560-600 tons.

Hledan Centre

Hledan Centre is a prominent shopping and office complex located in Kamayut Township, Yangon.Situated at coner of Pyay Road and Hledan Road.It's inside has Retail Mall, Supermarket, Co-working spaces, Beauty and Health,Dining options.Hledan Centre is part of AMC's broader development plans,contributing to Yangon's growing business and retail infrastructure.

Myanmar Plaza

Myanmar Praza ,popular shopping centre is located in Kabar Aye Pagoda Road,Yankin Township,Yangon.It is own by Hoang Anh Gia Lai, and opened in 2015.Inside Myanmar Plaza are shops & Brands, Restaurants, Supermarket ,entertainment. There is also a large LED billboard mounted on the plaza's facade,often used for advertising or announcements.

Inya lake

Inya lake is the largest lake in Yangon, Myanmar. It's located in the northern Yangon,and near Pyay Road , Inya Road and is a popular destination for both locals and tourists.The lake is known for its peaceful atmosphere, scenic beauty and recreational opportunities.It is built by British during the colonial era(constructed in 1882) as a water reservoir to supply water to Yangon city.

Famous Local Dishes!

Malahotpot

Mala Hotpot is a spicy Chinese-style hotpot that originated from Sichuan Province,China.Ma is a numbing sensation from Sichuan peppercorns and La is spicy flavor from dried chili peppers.It's famous for its bold,spicy and numbing taste and is very popular in Yangon.Common ingredients are meats,seafood,vegetables,Tofu and Noodles.

Suki Soup,known as Thai Suki is a popular Thai-style hotpot dish where diners cook a variety of ingredients in a flavorful clear broth then dip them into a special sukiyaki sauce.It's lighter and sweeter compared to Chinese Mala Hotpot.Common ingredients in Suki Soup are meats ,seafood ,vegetables , Noodles, Tofu and egg.

Suki Soup

ShabuShabu

Shabu Shabu is a popular Japanese hotpot dish. The name "Shabu Shabu" come from the sound made when thin slices of meat are swished through boiling broth. Main ingredients in Shabu Shabu are thinly sliced beef or pork, a variety of vegetables (like mushrooms ,Chinese cabbage, and carrots),tofu and noodles.

Yangon Mohinga is one of the most iconic and beloved traditional Myanmar dishes. While mohinga is eaten throught Myanmar, the version found in Yangon often has its own special taste and character.Common ingredients in Mohinga are soft rice noodles ,fish-based soup (usually with catfish or dried fish) Dried shrimp or fried fritters ,sliced boiled egg, banana stem, lime juice, coriander leaves, optional chili flakes, crispy garlic oil, or chickpea flour to thicken.
