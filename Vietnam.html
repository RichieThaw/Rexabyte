Famous Places In Vietnam

Ha Long Bay(Cat Ba Island)

Ha Long Bay is a UNESCO World Heritage site in northeastern Vietnam, known for its emerald waters and thousands of towering limestone islands topped with rainforests. It’s one of Vietnam’s most iconic natural landmarks

Ninh Binh

If you’re looking for the most unique places to visit in Vietnam, climb to the top of Mua Caves for an amazing view. Their is a dragon wrapped across the top of this mountain. The hike is 500 steps and is best visited at sunset. Other unique things to do in Ninh Binh include a boat tour through Trang An and a visit to Bich Dong Pagoda.

SAPA

Sapa is one of the top places to visit in Vietnam, especially if you are into nature. The town is surrounded by stunning terraced rice fields and misty mountains. It’s still relatively considered as a hidden gem.

HA GIANG LOOP

The Ha Giang Loop is one of the most incredible places to go in Vietnam and it’s untouched by tourists. This thrilling motorbike route takes you through rugged mountains, deep valleys, and breathtaking rice terrace. The Loop is an escape into nature, away from all the tourist crowds.

Phu Quoc Island

Phu Quoc Island is one of the best places to visit in Vietnam, especially if you want to get a glimpse of Vietnam’s pristine beaches. Come here for the laid-back vibe and the delicious food.

Quang phu Cau

Quảng Phú Cầu is a small village in Vietnam known for its centuries-old incense-making craft. you’ll find bundles of bright red incense sticks, often spread out in the sun to dry. I’d recommend visiting on a tour as it can be tricky to find the right places to go if you just get dropped off by a cab.

Famous Local Foods In Vietnam

Com Tay Cam

Com Tay Cam is a fun Vietnamese dish. It involves slow-cooking rice in a clay pot. The rice on the bottom becomes crispy and golden, contrasting with the softer grains on top.

Mi Quang

Mi Quang is a noodle dish from the Quang Nam province in Central Vietnam. Unlike other Vietnamese noodle soups, which use a generous amount of broth, Mi Quang is known for its minimal use of liquid, resulting in more concentrated and bold flavors.

Goi Ga

Originating from the northern regions of Vietnam, goi ga, or Vietnamese chicken salad, is a popular food often enjoyed during festivals and family gatherings. Common ingredients include shredded chicken, thinly sliced cabbage, and an array of fresh herbs, such as mint and cilantro.

Bo Luc Lac

Bo Luc Lac translates to "beef that has been shaken," which makes sense since the beef cubes are tossed quickly in a hot pan or wok.

Canh Chua

Canh chua, often called Vietnamese sour soup, is a flavorful dish from the Mekong Delta in southern Vietnam. This tangy, sweet, and spicy soup showcases abundant produce and seafood in the region.
