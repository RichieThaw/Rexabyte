Famous Places In Cambodia

Royal Palace of Cambodia

The Royal Palace of Cambodia in Phnom Penh is a grand complex serving as the royal residence and a symbol of Khmer architecture, history, and culture.It is located in the capital city of Phnom Penh and acts as the residence for the King of Cambodia.

Koh Rong

Koh Rong offers an incredible mix of stunning beaches, vibrant underwater life, thrilling adventures, and memorable cultural experiences, making it a top destination for both relaxation and excitement.

Phnom Penh Museum

The National Museum of Cambodia in Phnom Penh is home to one of the world’s greatest collections of Khmer art, housed in a beautifully designed traditional Khmer-style building located near the Royal Palace.

Angkor Wat Temple

Siem Reap is a vibrant city famous as the gateway to the iconic Angkor Wat temple complex, offering rich cultural heritage, bustling markets, and notable historical attractions.Angkor Wat is the heart of the Angkor Archaeological Park and stands as an iconic symbol of Cambodia.

Battambang Bat Caves

The Battambang Bat Caves near Phnom Sampov are home to millions of Asian Wrinkle-Lipped Free-Tailed Bats, creating a mesmerizing natural nightly event that draws visitors to witness their mass exodus at dusk.

Central Market(Phsar Thmei)

A landmark with stunning Art Deco architecture, it hosts jewelry, electronics, clothes, souvenirs, and a lively fresh food section including seafood and Cambodian delicacies. Popular with tourists but still maintains a lively local vibe.

Famous Local Foods In Cambodia

Amok (Coconut fish curry)

Amok trey is Cambodia’s national dish, a fragrant and spicy coconut fish curry tenderly steamed in banana leaves, which gives it a mousse-like texture which all but makes it melt in the mouth. The blended spice paste, kroeung, is added to the dish. Chicken, tofu, and snails can be used as a substitute for the fish.

Kuy Teav Noodle Soup

Kuy Teav is a pork bone noodle soup and a breakfast favorite in Cambodia. It is usually made from rice vermicelli and beef or pork bones. The broth is flavored with fried shallots, green onion, garlic and bean sprouts. Pork or beef balls are then added just before serving.

Samlar Machu(sour soup)

Samlar Machu, meaning "sour soup" in Khmer, is a classic Cambodian dish that combines a balance of sour, sweet, salty, and umami flavors, typically using tamarind or lime as souring agents alongside fresh herbs and seasonal vegetables.

Kampot Pepper Crab

Kampot Pepper Crab is a celebrated Cambodian stir-fried crab dish that perfectly marries the region’s famous Kampot pepper with the sweet, fresh flavors of local crab, showcasing unique flavor harmony in Khmer cuisine.

Nom banh chok(“Khmer noodles”)

Nom banh chok(“Khmer noodles”) is a quintessential Cambodian breakfast dish you’ll find at virtually every street corner around the morning rush hour. The dish comprises a fish curry served over a bed of fresh rice noodles, wild herbs, and vegetables.
