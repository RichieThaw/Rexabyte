Famous Places In Laos

Vietiane

Vietiane, the largest and capacital city of Laos, mixes French-colonial architecture with Buddhist temples such as the gloden, 16th-century Pha That Luang, which is national symbol. Situated on the banks of the Mekang River at the Thai border.

Luang Prabang

The ancient capital of Luang Prabang Province in nothern Laos, lies in a valley at the confluence of the Melong ang Nam Kham river. The royal capital of the country until 1975. It's known for its many Buddhist temples including the gilded Wat Xieng Thong and Wat Mai.

Vang Vieng

Vang Vieng is a small town north of Vientien on the Nam Song River in Laos. It's surrounded by striking limestone mountains and caves.At West, Tham Poukham is a cave with a blue-green lagoon and reclining Buddha status.At North, Tham Nam Cave, which has a spring at its entrance.

Plain of Jars

The Plain of Jars is a megalithic archaeological landscape in Laos.It consists of thousands of stone jars scattered aroung the upland valleys and the lower foothills of the central plain of the Xiangkhoang Plateau.

Bolaven Plateau

The Bolaven Plateau is a large, elevated region in southern Laos, known for its cool climate, stunning waterfalls, and coffee plantations.It is located between the Annamite Range, along which runs Laos' eastern border with Vietnam.

Si Phan Don

Si Phan Don(4000 islands) is a riverine archipelago in the Mekong River, Champasak Province in southern Laos. Si Phan Don is part of Khong District, including the islands and part of the mainland in the east. Si Phan Don is dotted with numerous islands

Famous Local Foods In Laos

Larb

Larb, also spelled laab, laap, larp, or lahb, is a minced meat salad in Lao cuisine. Known for its bold and harmonious flavors, it is often accompanied by sticky rice and green papaya salad.

Tam Mak Hoong

Tam mak hoong is a popular Lao dish that is fast becoming well known around the world for its unique spiciness and flavor.This crunchy salad packs in a lot of flavour and is made with shredded green papaya, dried shrimp, tomatoes, and sometimes carrots and beans.

Khao Niao

Khao niao, meaning "sticky rice" in Lao, is the most important staple food in Laos. It's a cultural cornerstone, deeply ingrained in Lao identity and traditions. This type of rice is steamed and typically served in a woven bamboo basket, often eaten by hand, and accompanies almost every meal.

Khao Piak Sen

Khao piak sen is a rice noodle soup that is a part of traditional Lao cuisine. It is a common comfort food that's great for a cold day.It is the "chicken noodle soup" of Lao cuisine and does not require too many ingredients, but makes a perfect dish with its simplicity.

Sai Oua

Sai Oua, also known as Lao sausage or Sai Gok, is a flavorful, traditionally made sausage from Laos, featuring ground pork, herbs, and spices.It can include coarsely chopped fatty pork seasoned with lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, shallots, cilantro, chilies, garlic, salt, sticky rice and fish sauce.
