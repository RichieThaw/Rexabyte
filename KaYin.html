Welcome to Kayin!

Kayin State in southeastern Myanmar is known for its stunning limestone mountains, sacred caves, and rich cultural heritage. It is home to the Karen ethnic group, whose traditions, language, and cuisine shape the region’s unique identity. The state offers a blend of spiritual sites, natural beauty, and local culture, making it a captivating place to explore.

Best Places To Go!

Taung Wine

Taung Wine Mountain stands tall near Hpa-An in Kayin State, offering one of the most breathtaking sunrise views in the region. The trail to the top is steep and lined with stairs, drawing both local pilgrims and adventurous hikers. At the summit, a small pagoda welcomes visitors with panoramic views of mist-covered hills and the winding Thanlwin River below. It’s a peaceful spot where nature and spirituality meet.

Kyauk Ka Lat

Kyauk Ka Lat Pagoda is a Buddhist temple located near Hpa-An in Kayin State. It stands on a tall limestone rock in the middle of a lake, with views of Mount Zwegabin. A footbridge connects the pagoda to the mainland, allowing visitors to walk across the water and explore the site.It is a peaceful location that attracts both pilgrims and travelers seeking quiet beauty, spiritual reflection, and panoramic views of the Kayin countryside.

Saddan Cave

Saddan Cave is a large Buddhist cave near Hpa-An, famous for hundreds of Buddha statues and natural formations. It is 107 meters long and 40 meters wide, the longest cave in Kayin State. Named after Saddan, the king of elephants, it features a large stalagmite called the "Stone Pillar of the King of Elephants." A lake at the cave’s exit is said to be his bathing spot. The Saddan Cave Pagoda Festival takes place every year during Thingyan.

Bayin Nyi Cave

Bayin Nyi Cave is a scenic limestone cave located near Hpa-An, surrounded by hot springs and lush greenery. Inside the cave are Buddhist shrines and Buddha images nestled among stalactites. A monastery is built into the cliff at the cave’s entrance, making it a peaceful religious site visited by locals and ravelers alike.

Kaw Gon Cave

Kawgun Cave is an ancient Buddhist site filled with thousands of small Buddha statues and carvings. Located near Hpa-An, the cave walls are covered with inscriptions and images dating back to the 7th century, making it a unique blend of history, art, and spirituality.

Yathae Pyan Cave

Yathe Pyin Cave is a limestone cave known for its impressive stalactites and stalagmites. It is a popular site for both tourists and locals, offering a peaceful atmosphere surrounded by natural beauty. The cave also holds cultural importance and is often visited for meditation and reflection. Its unique rock formations and tranquil environment make it a notable destination.

Mount Zwegabin

Mount Zwegabin is a prominent limestone mountain near Hpa-An in Kayin State. It rises about 722 meters and is known for its panoramic views and spiritual significance. A monastery and hundreds of Buddha statues sit near the summit, attracting pilgrims and hikers. T he mountain is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a scenic trek to the top.

Mount Kyar Inn

Tucked away from the tourist trail, Mount Kyar Inn offers a peaceful hike through lush greenery and calm air. It’s not as high or crowded as other peaks, but it rewards explorers with wide views, rich biodiversity, and a sense of untouched natural beauty. Ideal for nature lovers and those who want to explore Kayin State’s raw beauty in solitude.

Mount Hpan Pu

Mount Hpan Pu stands just outside Hpa An with a golden pagoda on top and panoramic views of the region. A 2.5 kilometer trail leads to the summit. According to local legend, a giant frog king gained magical powers after swallowing a ruby, sparking a battle with a dragon that gave Hpa An its unusual name.

Mount Mulayit

Mount Mulayit is a prominent peak in the Dawna Range, located in southeastern Kayin State near the Thai border. Standing at around 2,000 meters, it is known for its cool climate, rich biodiversity, and the revered Mulayit Pagoda at its summit. The mountain is a popular trekking and pilgrimage site, offering panoramic views and a peaceful atmosphere.

Mulayit Pagoda

Located on Mount Mulayit in Kayin State near the Thai border, Mulayit Pagoda is a revered Buddhist site believed to house a sacred relic of the Buddha. The peaceful hilltop setting offers stunning views, rare wildlife, and is a popular pilgrimage destination.

Thanlwin Bridge

Thanlwin Bridge is an important road bridge that crosses the Thanlwin River near Hpa-An in Kayin State. It connects the town to other regions and plays a key role in transportation and trade. The bridge offers scenic views of the river and surrounding mountains, making it not only functional but also a beautiful landmark in the area.

Taung Tha Mhan

Taung Tha Mhan Bridge is a scenic and peaceful wooden bridge stretching across paddy fields near Hpa-An. Known for its calm atmosphere and beautiful sunset views, the bridge is popular among locals and travelers alike. Whether you're walking across or simply admiring the view, it offers a quiet moment in nature with the Zwegabin Mountain often visible in the distance.

Htunt Patine Lake

Htunt Patine Lake is a quiet natural lake surrounded by lush greenery in Kayin State, not far from Hpa-An. The still waters reflect the sky and nearby hills, making it a peaceful spot for visitors seeking relaxation or quiet exploration. Locals often visit for picnics, and the area offers a serene escape from the busier tourist routes.

Loon Nya Lake

Hidden among limestone cliffs, Loon Nya Blue Lake is a surreal pool of crystal-clear water tinted with a deep turquoise glow. Surrounded by lush greenery and karst formations, the lake feels like a secret oasis untouched by time. It’s a perfect spot for quiet reflection, scenic photos, or simply soaking in the natural wonder.

Glu Taw Waterfall

Tucked within the green hills of Kayin State, Glu Taw Waterfall roars to life as it cascades down rugged cliffs into a cool misty pool below. The sound of rushing water echoes through the forest, inviting hikers and nature lovers to explore its refreshing beauty.

Shwe Yin Myaw

Shwe Yin Myaw Pagoda is a riverside temple located in Hpa-An. Famous for its stunning sunset views over the Thanlwin River, the pagoda features mythical creatures and local legends. It’s a peaceful spot for both worship and relaxation.

Lum Bini Garden

Lumbini Garden is a sacred site located in Hpa-An, Kayin State. It sits at the base of Mount Zwegabin and is known for its serene atmosphere and spiritual significance. The garden features a long row of hundreds of identical Buddha statues lined symmetrically along a walkway, creating a striking and peaceful visual. It is a popular spot for both pilgrims and visitors who come to enjoy the natural beauty and tranquility of the area.

Chit Thu Myaing

Chit Thu Myaing Resort is a peaceful retreat surrounded by gardens and gentle hills, offering a calm atmosphere ideal for rest and relaxation. Located not far from Hpa An, the resort features traditional-style villas, a scenic lake, and quiet paths where guests can enjoy the beauty of nature. It’s a popular spot for families and travelers looking to unwind in comfort while staying close to Kayin State’s natural attractions.

Mya Sandi Park

Mya Sandi Public Park is a well-known recreational area located in Myawadi, Kayin State. The park offers a calm and green environment where families and visitors can relax and enjoy outdoor activities. It features open spaces, benches, and walking paths surrounded by trees and flowers. The park is often used for community gatherings and cultural events, making it a popular spot in the town.

Famous Local Dishes!

Talabaw Soup

Karen Talabaw is a traditional Karen soup made from bamboo shoots, fish, and forest herbs. Once used to stretch rice in hard times, it’s now a proud symbol of Karen culture, known for its rich, earthy flavor and deep roots in village life.

Ta Heh Phoe is a traditional dish of the S’gaw Karen people, often served similarly to mohinga, with rice noodles in a rich, flavorful broth. It typically includes dried shrimp or dried fish, along with local herbs, leaves, and vegetables. Ta Heh Phoe reflects the Karen people's deep connection with nature and their culinary creativity using local resources.

Ta Heh Phoe

Kayin Mohinga

Kayin Mohinga is a regional twist on Myanmar’s national dish. Unlike the classic version, it’s served with either a sweet or spicy broth. This variation includes raw tomatoes, bean sprouts, green beans, and mint, giving it a fresh and vibrant flavor. It still retains the base of rice noodles in a fish-based broth infused with lemongrass and turmeric.

Karen Chicken Salad is a fresh and flavorful dish made with shredded chicken, toasted rice powder, lime juice, chili, and herbs like mint. It’s a light, traditional salad popular in Karen cuisine.

Chicken Salad

Steamed Fish Parcel

Karen Steamed Fish Parcel is a traditional dish where seasoned fish is wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. It’s infused with herbs like lemongrass, garlic, and chili, giving it a fragrant, delicate flavor. Often served with rice, this dish is known for its light yet flavorful taste and is a staple in Karen festive meals.

Karen Fish Cake Curry is a flavorful dish made with handmade fish cakes simmered in a mildly spicy, aromatic curry. The curry is typically prepared with garlic, onions, turmeric, and local herbs, creating a rich and hearty flavor.

Fish Cake Curry
