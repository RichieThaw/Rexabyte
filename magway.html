Welcome To Magway !

Magway Region is one of Myanmar’s central Regions, sharing borders with Chin and Rakhine, Bago, Mandalay and Naypyitaw, and Sagaing. Magway covers 44,819km2, and consists of 25 townships and the capital city is Magway. It is estimated population of 4.09 million, or 91 people per square kilometre. 15% of the population live in urban areas, and the remaining 85% in rural areas.

Best Places To Go !

Shwe Set Taw

Shwe Set Taw (The Golden Footprints) pagoda is one of the most venerated pagodas in Myanmar. Actually there are two pagodas: the Auk Set Taw Ya or the Lower and the Ahtet Set Taw Ya, the Upper.The pagoda history tells us that in the 12th year after Lord Buddha attained his Enlightenment He came to this area with a retinue of 500 followers. At that time the present Mann Chaung (Mann Creek) was known as Namada and here lived a Dragon King.

Yoke Sone Kyaung

Yokesone Monastery is a historic Buddhist monastery in Sale, Magwe Region, Myanmar. The wooden monastery, which dates to 1882, is known for its abundance of woodcarvings which depict scenes of the Jataka tales.The monastery was constructed with 153 teak posts, each more than 3 feet in diameter, which hold the building up about 10 feet above the ground, during the time of King Thibaw.

NagaPwekVolcano

Naga Pwek Volcano, also known as Naga Pwek Taung (Mountain where the Dragon Breathes), is a mud volcano located in Minbu, central Myanmar. It's a geological feature that locals historically associated with the mythical dragon (naga), believing it to be the source of fiery breath. While scientifically classified as a mud volcano, its unique appearance and association with local folklore have made it a notable landmark.

Man Paya Pagoda

Info Man Paya is located in Salay. The largest lacquer Buddha image in Myanmar. Man Paya, also known as Man Pagoda, is a Buddhist temple in Sale, Magway Region, Myanmar, famous for housing the largest lacquer Buddha image in Myanmar, the Shinbin Maha Laba Man Buddha. This image, said to date back to the 13th century, is approximately 20 feet tall and is believed to have floated from Monywa to Sale after flooding in 1888.

Mya Tha Lon

The Mya Tha Lun Pagoda is a Buddhist pagoda and the landmark of Magway which is located on the river side of the Irrawaddy.According to legend, the pagoda was initially built by a wealthy man called U Baw Gyaw and his wife. It was raised from its original height of 55.5 feet (16.9 m) to a height of 87 feet (27 m) by King Saw Lu (1077-1084) of Bagan.The pagoda faced a huge earthquake in 1847 and it was rebuilt by the mayor of Magway.

General Aung San

Situated in Natmauk Township, Magway Region, it is separate from the Bogyoke Aung San Museum in Yangon, which focuses on his adult life and political career. The General Aung San Residence Museum in Natmauk, Magway Region, is dedicated to the childhood and family memorabilia of Myanmar's national hero, General Aung San. It showcases his early life, including his education at a Buddhist monastery, his family, and personal belongings.

Beikthano

Beikthano is situated in the irrigated Magway Region, near present-day Taungdwingyi. In the era of the Pyu city-states it was a city of considerable significance, possibly a local capital replacing Sri Ksetra. Today the modest village is noted for its hot springs and archaeological sites. Beikthano, Hanlin, and Sri Ksetra, the ancient cities of the Pyu Kingdom were built on the irrigated fields of the dry zone of the Ayeyawady River basin.

Minhla Fortress

Minhla Fortress is a historic fortress located in Minhla, Magway Region, Myanmar.The fortress was built in 1860 to 1861 to defend the Konbaung dynasty's territory following the Second Anglo-Burmese War.It was built with the assistance of French and Italian engineers, including Captain Molinari, an Italian engineer,along the western bank of the Irrawaddy River.

Tant Kyi Taung

Tantkyitaung Pagoda located across the Ayeyarwady River in Pakokku District, Magway Division, Myanmar (Burma) is a prominent Burmese Buddhist pagoda near the historic city of Bagan, believed to enshrine four tooth relics of Gautama Buddha.It was built by King Anawrahta in 397 ME on Tantkyi Hill.

Local Famous Dishes !

Palm cake

Palm tree is the symbol of tropical region and many grow at the central area. Palm cake is the traditional cake of Burma. Mainly around Yesagyo local community made and their palm cakes are popular more than in other regions. Palm is made with combination of palm cream, sugar, egg, coconut cream, rice power, and water. Local community of central region made it.

Htamanè

Htamanè pronounced is a glutinous rice-based savory snack, and a seasonal festive delicacy in Myanmar. The traditional delicacy is ceremonially prepared around and on the full moon day of Tabodwe, the 11th lunar month on the traditional Burmese calendar (roughly in February), just as the cool season ends. Some pagodas and monasteries, including the Shwedagon Pagoda, hold htamane-making competitions.

Fried Peanut

Fried peanut fritters are a traditional Myanmar snack made primarily from peanuts. They are a delicious, crispy, and easy-to-make treat that can be enjoyed at home or bought from local street vendors.he preparation involves slightly boiling the peanuts, then mixing them in a batter made with chickpea flour or rice flour, seasoned with spices such as turmeric, chili powder, and salt. The mixture is then deep-fried until golden and crispy

Hsi htamin is a traditional Burmese snack or mont, popularly served as a breakfast dish, often served alongside peas or dried fish.The dish consists of glutinous rice cooked with turmeric, salt, and onions, and served with roasted sesame seeds and fried onions, which renders a golden hue to the rice.
