Best Places to go!

Mount Victoria

The highest peak in Chin State and the third-highest in Myanmar, Mount Victoria stands at 3,053 meters. It is a popular trekking destination and part of the Nat Ma Taung National Park. The mountain is famous for its breathtaking views, rare orchids, and diverse bird species. It's a must-visit for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Mindat

Mindat is known for its rich Chin culture and stunning mountain scenery. Visitors can explore traditional Chin villages and meet local women who still carry the unique tradition of facial tattoos. The town also offers hiking opportunities and panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

Kanpetlet

A quiet and beautiful town near Mount Victoria, Kanpetlet is another gateway for treks to the mountain. It has charming guesthouses and offers an authentic rural experience. The area is covered with pine forests and rolling hills, perfect for peaceful getaways.

Hakha

Hakha is the capital of Chin State and a great place to experience the local way of life. The town sits on a mountain ridge with fantastic views. Visitors can explore churches, small markets, and witness the cultural life of the Chin people.

Rih Lake

Located on the Myanmar-India border, Rih Lake is one of the most beautiful natural lakes in Myanmar. The heart-shaped lake is surrounded by hills and forests, and is considered sacred by locals. It's an ideal spot for photography, picnics, and peaceful walks.

Mt. Kennedy

Kennedy Peak is the second-highest mountain in Chin State, located near Tedim. It is a significant historical site, as it played a role during World War II. Adventurers can enjoy trekking to the summit for sweeping views of Chin hills and beyond.

Thantlang

Thantlang is a charming mountain town famous for its cool climate and scenic beauty. It is surrounded by green hills and is known for its friendly locals and traditional Chin lifestyle. Hikers will enjoy exploring the nearby trails and viewpoints.

Famous Local Dishes!

Sabuti

Sabuti is one of the most beloved traditional foods from Chin State. It is a warm and filling noodle soup often served during festivals and family gatherings. The soup is made with flat rice noodles and a rich broth, topped with meat and crunchy beans. Its comforting flavor and mild spices make it popular among all age groups. The ingredients of Sabuti include: Flat rice noodles, beef or chicken, beans, garlic, ginger, onions, spring onions, chili powder, and cooking oil.

Bamboo Shoot Curry is a well-known dish in Chin hills, often enjoyed for its earthy flavor and slight tanginess. Bamboo shoots are cooked with local herbs and meat, creating a hearty curry packed with aroma. It is perfect with plain rice.The ingredients of Bamboo Shoot Curry include:Fresh bamboo shoots, pork or chicken, onions, garlic, tomatoes, fermented soybeans (optional), chili powder, turmeric, salt, and oil.

Bamboo Shoot Curry

Pumpkin Curry

Pumpkin Curry is a simple yet delicious dish often cooked in Chin households. It has a creamy texture with a subtle sweetness from pumpkin, balanced with mild spices. This humble dish is loved for its homely flavor. The ingredients of Pumpkin Curry include: Pumpkin pieces, onions, garlic, chili powder, oil, salt, and sometimes small dried shrimp for extra flavor.

Hkaung Rany is a famous traditional alcoholic drink from Chin State. It is made by fermenting rice or millet and is an essential part of festivals, weddings, and social gatherings. This strong local liquor reflects the rich cultural heritage of the Chin people. The taste can vary from mildly sweet to strong and sharp, depending on how long it is fermented.The ingredients of Hkaung Rany include:Rice or millet, yeast, and water.

Hkaung Rany

Sa Bu Si

Sa Bu Si is a sweet Chin snack made from sticky rice and jaggery. It is popular during special occasions and is loved for its chewy texture and caramel-like sweetness. The ingredients of Sa Bu Si include: Sticky rice flour, jaggery (palm sugar), grated coconut, sesame seeds.

Sweet potatoes are widely grown in Chin hills and are enjoyed roasted, boiled, or made into snacks. They are nutritious, naturally sweet, and serve as an important food source for locals, especially in the highlands.The ingredients of Sweet Potato dish include:Sweet potatoes (boiled or roasted), salt (optional), and sometimes served with jaggery or sesame seeds.
