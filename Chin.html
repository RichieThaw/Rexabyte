Welcome To Chin!

Explore the hidden beauty of Myanmar’s mountain paradise! Chin State invites you with its cool climate, stunning hills, and rich culture. Discover majestic peaks like Mount Victoria, peaceful Rih Lake, and meet the friendly Chin people known for their unique traditions. Adventure, nature, and culture await you here. Come and experience the untouched charm of Chin State!

Best Places to go!

Mount Victoria

The highest peak in Chin State and the third-highest in Myanmar, Mount Victoria stands at 3,053 meters. It is a popular trekking destination and part of the Nat Ma Taung National Park. The mountain is famous for its breathtaking views, rare orchids, and diverse bird species. It's a must-visit for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Mindat

Mindat is known for its rich Chin culture and stunning mountain scenery. Visitors can explore traditional Chin villages and meet local women who still carry the unique tradition of facial tattoos. The town also offers hiking opportunities and panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

Kanpetlet

A quiet and beautiful town near Mount Victoria, Kanpetlet is another gateway for treks to the mountain. It has charming guesthouses and offers an authentic rural experience. The area is covered with pine forests and rolling hills, perfect for peaceful getaways.

Hakha

Hakha is the capital of Chin State and a great place to experience the local way of life. The town sits on a mountain ridge with fantastic views. Visitors can explore churches, small markets, and witness the cultural life of the Chin people.

Rih Lake

Located on the Myanmar-India border, Rih Lake is one of the most beautiful natural lakes in Myanmar. The heart-shaped lake is surrounded by hills and forests, and is considered sacred by locals. It's an ideal spot for photography, picnics, and peaceful walks.

Mt. Kennedy

Kennedy Peak is the second-highest mountain in Chin State, located near Tedim. It is a significant historical site, as it played a role during World War II. Adventurers can enjoy trekking to the summit for sweeping views of Chin hills and beyond.

Thantlang

Thantlang is a charming mountain town famous for its cool climate and scenic beauty. It is surrounded by green hills and is known for its friendly locals and traditional Chin lifestyle. Hikers will enjoy exploring the nearby trails and viewpoints.

Famous Local Dishes!

Sabuti

A beloved Chin noodle soup, warm and hearty, often enjoyed at festivals and family gatherings.

Bamboo Shoot Curry

Earthy and tangy bamboo shoots cooked with local herbs and meat, a Chin hills favorite.

Pumpkin Curry

Creamy, mildly spiced pumpkin curry, a simple yet comforting staple in Chin households.

Hkaung Rany

A traditional Chin rice or millet wine, central to festivals, weddings, and social gatherings.

Sa Bu Si

Sa Bu Si is a Chin sweet made from sticky rice and jaggery, loved for its chewy texture and caramel-like flavor.

Chin Sweet Potatoes

Chin sweet potatoes, roasted, boiled, or as snacks, are a sweet, nutritious highland staple.
