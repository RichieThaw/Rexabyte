About Kalaw

Located at an elevation of 1,320 meters in the southern Shan State, Kalaw was established as a hill station by British civil servants fleeing the heat of the plains. Today, it's known for its cool climate, scenic beauty, and trekking opportunities.

The town's unique charm comes from its blend of colonial architecture, Buddhist sites, and proximity to traditional hill tribe villages. The surrounding hills are covered with pine forests, tea plantations, and terraced fields.

Kalaw serves as the starting point for one of Myanmar's most popular trekking routes - the multi-day hike to Inle Lake. This journey takes you through stunning landscapes and offers authentic cultural encounters with local ethnic groups.

Travel Tips for Kalaw

Make the most of your visit to Kalaw with these essential travel tips

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time is from October to February when the weather is cool and dry. Avoid the rainy season (June to September).

Trekking Essentials

Bring sturdy walking shoes, layered clothing, rain gear, sunscreen, and insect repellent for your trekking adventures.

Local Cuisine

Try traditional Shan noodles, tea leaf salad, and local specialties like stuffed bamboo shoots and mountain rice.

Cultural Respect

Dress modestly, remove shoes before entering religious sites, and always ask permission before photographing people.
