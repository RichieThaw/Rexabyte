Welcome To Rakhine !

Rakhine State is one of the most historically and culturally rich regions of Myanmar, located along the western coast, bordering the Bay of Bengal and Bangladesh. It is known for its ancient kingdoms, beautiful beaches, traditional food, and unique culture.

Best Places To Go !

Shittaung Pagoda

Shittaung Pagoda is the most famous and iconic temple in Mrauk U, Rakhine State. It is a major religious site, historical monument, and tourist attraction, often called the "Temple of 80,000 Buddha Images".

Koe Thaung

Kouthaung is the largest Buddhist temple in Mrauk U, Myanmar. The name means "Temple of 90,000 Buddha Images". The temple was built between 1554 and 1556 by King Dikkha

Ngapali

Ngapali Beach is Myanmar’s premier coastal escape, nestled on the Bay of Bengal in Rakhine State, about 7 km from Thandwe. With its pristine white sands, swaying palms, and turquoise waters, it's a low‑key paradise—less crowded than Southeast Asia's popular beaches .

Ramree Island

Ramree Island, also known as Yanbye Island, is Myanmar’s largest island, spanning approximately 1,350 square kilometers. Located off the coast of Rakhine State, it is connected to the mainland by a narrow isthmus and features diverse landscapes, including mangrove forests, hills, and beaches.

Gwa Beach

Gwa Beach is a quiet, beautiful beach located in Gwa Township, southern Rakhine State, Myanmar. It’s known for its peaceful, untouched white sandy shores and palm trees, perfect for travelers seeking solitude away from popular tourist spots like Ngapali.

Yoma

The Rakhine Yoma, also known as the Rakhine Mountains, is a significant mountain range that runs along the western coast of Myanmar, forming a natural barrier between Rakhine State and the country's central regions.

Sittwe

Sittwe, formerly known as Akyab, is the capital city of Rakhine State, located at the mouth of the Kalathe River, where it flows into the Bay of Bengal. It serves as the main port, administrative, and commercial center of western Myanmar.

Local Famous Dishes !

Mont di

Rakhine's most famous noodle dish is Mont Di. It is a light yet flavorful rice noodle mixed with spicy fish broth and special Rakhine chili paste. Locals love it for its simplicity and bold, fresh flavors. Mont Di is known for its strong seafood aroma and sour-spicy taste. The ingredients of Mont Di include: Thin rice noodles, boiled fish, fermented fish paste (Nga Pi), chili paste, garlic oil, coriander leaves, lime juice.

Ngapi Thoke is a popular traditional salad from Rakhine, made using the region's signature fermented fish paste. It has a salty, strong, and savory flavor with a spicy kick. This salad perfectly reflects the bold flavors of Rakhine cuisine.The ingredients of Ngapi Thoke include:Fermented fish paste (Nga Pi), onions, garlic, green chilies, lime juice, roasted peanuts, coriander leaves, cooking oil.

Nga-pi Thoke

Mont Let Saung

Mont Let Saung is a popular traditional dessert enjoyed all over Rakhine. It is served cold, making it the perfect treat for hot coastal days. The soft jelly noodles float in sweet coconut milk and palm sugar syrup. The ingredients of Mont Let Saung include: Rice flour jelly, palm sugar syrup, fresh coconut milk, salt, crushed ice.

Sticky Rice with Coconut is a simple yet delicious Rakhine dessert. Sweet and creamy coconut milk is poured over soft, glutinous rice, creating a comforting, mildly sweet flavor.The ingredients of Sticky Rice with Coconut include:Glutinous rice, coconut milk, sugar, salt, toasted sesame seeds or grated coconut (optional).

Sticky Rice Coconut

Sea Snail Curry

Sea Snail Curry is one of Rakhine’s traditional dishes. It is made with fresh sea snails simmered in a rich, spicy curry. The unique chewy texture of the sea snails combined with aromatic spices gives this dish a bold and unforgettable flavor. The ingredients of its: Fresh sea snails, onions, garlic, ginger, tomatoes, Rakhine chili paste, turmeric powder, fermented fish paste (Nga Pi), oil, salt, and coriander leaves.

Rakhine people love their dried fish dishes, which are often salty, spicy, and crispy. These dishes are enjoyed as snacks or side dishes with rice and reflect the state's rich fishing tradition.The ingredients of dried fish dishes include:Dried fish (various types), onions, garlic, chilies, cooking oil, lime, fried beans or peanuts (optional).
