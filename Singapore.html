top palces to visit

Merlion Park

The Merlion Park is one of the country’s sought-after tourist sites. As the name indicates, the park situates two statues of the Merlion, a mythical creature with a lion's head and the body and tail of a fish. Merlion represents Singapore's origin as a fishing village combined with its traditional Malay name Singapura which means "lion city", hence it is widely used as Singapore’s national mascot. Among two Merlion statues, the original one, which overlooks Marina Bay and also attracts more visitors, measures 8.6 meters tall and spouts water from its mouth.

location 📍 located in the Downtown Core district near Central Business District of Singapore.

Garden by the Bay

Garden by the Bay is a huge natural park and botanic garden in Singapore. Spanning an extensive area of 101 hectares, the garden is the home to approximately 400,000 plants, which is divided into three parts including Bay Central Garden, Bay East Garden, and Bay South Garden. Garden by the Bay is famous for its numerous eye-catching structures such as Supertree Grove, Flower Dome, Cloud Forest Dome, SkyWay, etc. Visiting here, people can admire futuristic green space, relish the vibrant plant life, and escape the city bustle for a while.

location 📍 located at 18 Marina Gardens Drive, Singapore 018953, adjacent to the Marina Reservoir and near the famous Marina Bay Sands hotel.

Sentosa Island

Sentosa is a beautiful island where offer endless fun from dawn till dusk for people of all age. Being the place of the sun, the sand, and the sea, Sentosa is not only famous for beach activities but also a paradise of amusement. The island houses Resorts World Sentosa, an integrated resort comprising diverse types of recreation such as casinos, world-famous theme park Universal Studios, water park, aquarium, playgrounds, art performances, etc besides luxury hotels & dining. Being a friendly tourist attraction to everyone, Sentosa island surely delivers amazing time for a great day out with your mates.

location 📍 Located not far away from the city centre to the South

China Town

Singapore Chinatown also offers a great blend of cultural diversity and it is the only Chinatown all over the world to have three different worship spots from three cultures: a Buddhist temple, a mosque and a Hindu temple along a single street. Tourists can easily gain insight into Singapore's richness of multi-ethnic and culture by strolling along the vibrant streets, visiting the prominent traditional sights, sampling different cuisines as well as encountering the trendy vibe from fancy cafeterias, shops, and diverse merriment here.

location 📍 located in the central part of the city within the Outram district, west of the Singapore River

Singapore cuisine

Laksa

Laksa is another popular noodle dish that’s a staple in Singapore food culture.Think of Laksa as the love child of spicy curry and noodle soup, born from Peranakan heritage.

Bak Kut Teh

Bak Kut Teh means meat bone tea, though there is no tea in the soup. This peppery pork rib soup is slow simmered with garlic, white pepper and herbs until the meat is fall off the bone tender. Add rice, fried dough sticks and preserved vegetables for a hearty meal.

Chilli Crab

Spicy, sweet, messy and glorious. Chilli Crab is the life of the Singaporean food party. Imagine fresh mud crab stir fried in a rich, tangy tomato chili gravy that is more savory than spicy. Served with soft steamed or crispy fried mantou buns to soak up the sauce, it is an absolute feast and a hands on experience.
